# Dataset Labeling Rules

## Styles

**Gothic** - Dark, alternative clothing with a stronger visual identity. Often includes black or muted tones, heavier materials, bold silhouettes, layered styling, or details associated with alternative fashion. It should look clearly distinct from formal, sporty, and streetwear.

**Formal** - Polished and structured clothing. Suitable for office, events, or dressier situations. Often cleaner and more refined in appearance.

**Sporty** - Athletic or activewear-inspired clothing. Items designed for sport or strongly associated with exercise and performance wear.

**Streetwear** - Urban, trend-driven clothing with a strong visual identity. May include logos, graphic prints, oversized cuts, or bold styling elements.

## Types

**Tshirts** - Upper-body clothing such as t-shirts, shirts, polos, and sweaters.

**Pants** - Lower-body clothing such as jeans, trousers, joggers, and leggings.

**Shoes** - Footwear such as sneakers, trainers, boots, and dress shoes.

**Jackets** - Outer-layer clothing such as jackets, blazers, coats, and zip jackets.

## Rules

1. Keep only images that clearly fit one folder.
2. If an item is too colourful or contains strong graphic prints, label it as _streetwear_.
3. If an item shows clearly dark, alternative, or visually heavy styling, label it as _gothic_.
4. Type is based on the clothing category itself, not on brand or styling details.
5. Style is based on visual appearance, not only on the website category.
6. Avoid ambiguous items whenever possible.
